Why a noreply@domain is bad

  1. Hurts email reputation and deliverability
  2. Confusing for both our users and us
  3. User's personal email settings are setup to send such noreply emails to junk
  4. Makes us look unapproachable
  5. ISPs don't allow email recipients to add nopreply to their address books

Tells our customers that we really don't care about what they have to say

noreply@submissions

All Submission Systems use the same noreply email to suggest to users, that they shouldn't reply to emails from the Drupal system. The email message however doesn't state this explicitly other than in the address itself, leading to a number of users replying to this email. A mailbox is configured for this email, however. It is upto us to explain to our customer that using a noreply email directly affects the sending domains reputation and any efforts made(IP & Domain whitelabel, etc.) are rendered useless.
